Introduction to Nakshi Kathas' :

Nakshi Katha, also known as Nakshi Kantha, is a traditional form of embroidered quilt or blanket that originated in the Bengal region, which spans across Bangladesh and the Indian state of West Bengal. "Nakshi" means artistic or decorative, while "Kantha" refers to a quilt or a stitched cloth. Nakshi Kathas are highly regarded for their intricate embroidery and rich cultural significance. Nakshi Kathas have a long history, dating back several centuries. They were traditionally created by rural women in Bengal, using discarded or worn-out fabrics such as old saris or dhotis. These fabrics were layered and stitched together using a simple running stitch called "kantha stitch." The patterns and motifs were then embroidered onto the quilt using colorful threads.
